Integer Constants or Integer literal


Is made up of an optional leading sign, one or more digits, and an optional suffix character called a radix indicating the number base.

Radix may be one of the following uppercase or lowercase
h Hexadecimal
q/o Octal
d Decimal
b Binary
r Encoded real
t Decimal altrenate
y Binary altrenate


Example


Here i will tell you examples of using different Radixes

11010011 Binary


Integer Expressions


Is a mathematical expression involving integer values and arithmetic operators.

The arithmetic operators are listed according to their precedence order, from highest 1 to lowest 4.

The important thing to realize about integer experssions is that they can only be evaluated at Assembly time. these are not runtime expression

Arithmetic Operators


Operators Name Precedence level
() Parentheses 1
+, - Unary plus, minus 2
*, / Multiply, divide 3
MOD Modulus 3


Precedence refers to the implied order of operator when an expression contains two or more operators.
The order of operations is show for the following expressions:
Mathematical equation Value Operators

12 - 1 MOD 2 11 Modulus, subtract
-1 + 10 9 Unary minus, add
